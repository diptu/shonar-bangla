# 📈 Economic Diversification

> *Reduce reliance on RMG and remittances. Build a broader, more resilient export base.*

It's 2026, and in a garment factory outside Gazipur, a machine operator hears the same worry she's heard for a decade: *what happens when the orders stop coming?* Eight of every ten dollars Bangladesh earns from the world still comes from clothing. One tariff shock, one global recession, one shift in buyer sentiment — and that hum of sewing machines goes quiet.

This is the story of how that changes.

## Chapter I — Laying New Ground (Years 1–3)

The first move isn't glamorous. It's paperwork, tax code, and concrete. Incentives roll out for pharmaceuticals, agro-processing, and light tech manufacturing. Two or three special economic zones rise outside the Dhaka–Chittagong corridor — in Khulna, in Rangpur, in places that have waited a long time for investment to look their way. Nothing dramatic happens yet. But the foundation is being poured.

## Chapter II — Finding a New Rhythm (Years 4–7)

By now, the zones are humming with something other than sewing machines. Electronics assembly lines. IT parks filling with young graduates who used to leave for Dhaka, or leave the country altogether. Bangladesh's BPO sector — already a quiet success story — scales into something the region notices. The economy starts breathing through more than one lung.

## Chapter III — A Different Kind of Made in Bangladesh (Years 8–10)

By year ten, the RMG share of exports has slipped below half — not because garments failed, but because everything else grew up around it. The machine operator from 2026 might still be sewing, or she might be running a small QA team at an electronics plant. Either way, the next global shock doesn't threaten to take everything down with it.

## The Obstacles

None of this survives on good intentions alone. It survives — or doesn't — on political continuity past election cycles, on how fast global trade policy shifts, and on whether the SEZs get built on schedule or stall in bureaucracy.

## The Numbers That Tell Us It's Working

- Export diversification index, rising
- Foreign investment outside RMG, rising
- Youth employment outside garments, rising
