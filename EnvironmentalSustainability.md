# ♻️ Environmental Sustainability

> *Growth that doesn't consume the ground it stands on.*

![Timeline](https://img.shields.io/badge/timeline-2026--2036-006A4E?style=flat-square)
![Status](https://img.shields.io/badge/status-proposed-F42A1D?style=flat-square)

A tannery worker in Hazaribagh — or what used to be Hazaribagh, before the industry relocated — knows better than most what unmanaged growth costs a river. Dhaka's air regularly ranks among the worst on Earth. Rivers that once fed cities now carry more industrial runoff than fish. Bangladesh's growth story has, too often, been written at the expense of the land and water underneath it.

This is the story of decoupling the two.

## Chapter I — Stopping the Bleeding (Years 1–3)

Industrial effluent standards get real enforcement, not just paper policy. Dhaka's worst air-quality sources — brick kilns, unregulated construction dust, vehicle emissions — face their first serious regulatory pressure. Waste segregation pilots launch in major cities, the unglamorous first step toward a circular economy.

## Chapter II — Building the Loop (Years 4–7)

Recycling and waste-to-energy infrastructure scales beyond pilot stage. Sustainable brick alternatives (compressed block, fly ash) start replacing the kilns that have blackened the sky around Dhaka for decades. River restoration projects bring the Buriganga and Turag back from the edge of ecological collapse.

## Chapter III — A Livable Baseline (Years 8–10)

Air quality in major cities meets WHO guidelines more days than not. Rivers near urban centers support fish again. The tannery worker's children, if they still live near the water, grow up next to something closer to what their grandparents remember.

## The Obstacles

Enforcement is where sustainability policy usually dies — regulations exist today that simply aren't applied. Industrial relocation displaces workers if it isn't paired with a transition plan. And circular-economy infrastructure needs consistent investment, not one-time grants.

## The Numbers That Tell Us It's Working

- Dhaka air quality index, improving
- River water quality near urban centers, improving
- Urban waste diverted from landfill, rising
