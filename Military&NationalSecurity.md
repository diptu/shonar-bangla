# 🛡️ Military & National Security

> *Modernize defense capability without letting it crowd out development.*

A naval officer patrolling the Bay of Bengal watches container ships pass on their way to ports that matter more every year — as Bangladesh's economy grows, so does what's worth protecting at sea, at the border, and increasingly, online. Security here has never just meant soldiers at a border. It means keeping the sea lanes that carry the country's trade safe, and keeping the systems that run its government safe from a different kind of intrusion.

This is the story of a defense posture built for the next ten years, not the last one.

## Chapter I — Knowing the Terrain (Years 1–3)

Investment shifts toward maritime domain awareness in the Bay of Bengal — radar, patrol capacity, coordination with regional partners — alongside a serious first build-out of cyber defense for government and financial infrastructure. The border isn't just a line on a map anymore; part of it is a network.

## Chapter II — Standing on Its Own (Years 4–7)

Domestic defense manufacturing grows — uniforms, basic equipment, eventually more — reducing reliance on imports for routine needs. UN peacekeeping deployments, already a point of quiet international credibility for Bangladesh, expand into a deliberate soft-power asset rather than an ad hoc contribution.

## Chapter III — A Deterrent, Not a Drain (Years 8–10)

By year ten, defense spending stays disciplined relative to GDP — modern enough to deter, not so large it starves schools and hospitals of funding. Cyber resilience is tested and proven, not theoretical. The officer on patrol in year one is, by now, training the next generation on systems built at home.

## The Obstacles

Defense budgets are politically sensitive and easy to pad or hide. Regional tensions can force priorities to shift overnight. And cyber capability decays fast if it isn't continuously reinvested in.

## The Numbers That Tell Us It's Working

- Defense spending as % of GDP, stable and disciplined
- Cyber incident response time, falling
- Domestic share of defense procurement, rising
