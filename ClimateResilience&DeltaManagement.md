# 🌊 Climate Resilience & Delta Management

> *Protect a low-lying delta nation from rising seas, cyclones, and river erosion.*

A family in a char village on the Jamuna has rebuilt their home three times in ten years. The river doesn't ask permission before it takes land. In the south, a cyclone warning goes out in a language and a timeframe that don't always reach the people who need it most. Bangladesh sits at the mouth of three of the world's great rivers, in the path of the Bay of Bengal's temper — geography as both blessing and constant test.

This is the story of learning to live with the water instead of losing to it.

## Chapter I — Reading the Water (Years 1–3)

The groundwork of Delta Plan 2100 finally gets teeth: embankments reinforced, tidal river management modernized, and — critically — an early-warning system that reaches every coastal upazila, not just the well-connected ones. The goal isn't to stop the water. It's to know what it's going to do before it does it.

## Chapter II — Building to Move (Years 4–7)

In the char lands, something new appears: floating and amphibious housing, built to rise with the flood instead of fighting it. For families who do have to leave, migration corridors are no longer accidental — they're planned, so climate migrants land somewhere with jobs and services instead of funneling into Dhaka's already-strained slums.

## Chapter III — A Coastline That Bends, Not Breaks (Years 8–10)

Salinity-resistant rice varieties are standard in coastal fields, not experimental. The mangrove belt — nature's own seawall — has roughly doubled. The family on the Jamuna may still rebuild someday. But the system around them, this time, is built to help them rebuild faster, and less often.

## The Obstacles

Financing this at scale is the hard part, not the engineering. Embankments need maintenance long after the ribbon-cutting. And migration corridors only work if they're governed fairly, not politically.

## The Numbers That Tell Us It's Working

- Climate-displaced population, falling
- Coastal crop yield stability, rising
- Mangrove coverage, rising
